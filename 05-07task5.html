"Sanam Teri Kasam" tells the story of Saraswati "Saru" Parthasarthy, a librarian who is disowned by her family after a misunderstanding, and Inder Parihaar, a brooding and seemingly tough neighbor. When Saru's family disowns her, she seeks help from Inder, who assists her in transforming herself and navigating a new life. As they spend time together, Inder falls in love with Saru, but she is set to marry someone else. However, tragedy strikes when Saru is left at the altar and later discovers she has a terminal illness. Inder supports her through her illness, and she makes a final wish for her burial under a tree. The movie explores themes of love, sacrifice, and the enduring nature of love despite tragedy Sanam Teri Kasam (transl. Swear on you, my beloved) is a 2016 Indian Hindi-language romantic drama film written and directed by Radhika Rao and Vinay Sapru. Produced by Deepak Mukut, the film stars Harshvardhan Rane and Mawra Hocane in their Hindi film debuts, with Anurag Sinha, Manish Chaudhari, Murli Sharma and Sudesh Berry. The film is a modern rendition of the legends of Shiva-Sati and novel Love Story by Eric Segal.[10][11] It tells the story of a tough ex-convict, Inder, and a Telugu librarian, Saru. They fall in love and get married but a tragedy changes their lives forever.

About

Saraswati "Saru" Parthasaarthy, a traditional Telugu librarian, is rejected by suitors who find her old-fashioned. Her sister Kaveri's wedding is postponed until Saru marries. When Kaveri threatens to elope, Saru promises to find a husband soon. Inder Lal Parihaar, an ex-convict known for his tough attitude, moves into the same building. The original owner, Lily, has already transferred ownership to Inder. Inder immediately falls for Saru. He breaks up with his girlfriend, Ruby Malhotra. Saru and Inder meet in the elevator by accident and later at the library where Saru works. At the library, Inder tries to pass written messages to Saru multiple times, but she overlooks them all the time. Saru secretly meets Inder to get a makeover from Ruby, hoping to impress her colleague Abhimanyu. A drunk Ruby barges in, misunderstands the situation, and throws a wine bottle at Saru. Inder shields Saru and gets injured. As they leave for treatment, the security guard sees them and assumes theyare intimate, alerting the neighbors. Saru tends to Inder's wounds. Later, Jayram (Saru's father) and neighbors find Saru in Inder's bed, misinterpreting the scene. Jayram, embarrassed, disowns Saru. Saru confides in her mother but asks her to keep it secret for Kaveri's marriage's sake.

Production

Development In 2015, the director duo Radhika Rao and Vinay Sapru announced the film, that would be distributed by Eros International.[15] The film was inspired from the classic legends of Shiva and his consort Sati. Sanam Teri Kasam is also inspired from Erich Segal's bestseller novel, Love Story.[16] The film derive its name from a song of the 1982 Hindi film Sanam Teri Kasam.[17] Casting Sanam Teri Kasam marked Rane and Hocane's Hindi film debut Harshvardhan Rane was cast to play Inder, an alcoholic lawyer. Rane termed the film and his role a "dream come true".[18] Rane said that he modeled his character on Salman Khan and his father, and added, "I’ve modeled my character in Sanam Teri Kasam on my father who is stern on the outside but all heart inside. I couldn’t be any luckier than this."[19] Pakistani actress Mawra Hocane was cast to play Saraswati, a South Indian librarian. Hocane said that she is "extremely grateful" to be a part of the film. She added, "I did Sanam Teri Kasam because there was scope to perform. I played the nerdy, introvert who transforms into the quintessential Bollywood heroine."[20] The film mark both the leads bollywood debut.[21] Manish Chaudhari and Pyumori Mehta Ghosh were cast as Saru's parents and Divyetta Singh as Saru's sister. Sudesh Berry was cast as Inder's father. Murli Sharma was cast as Inspector, Anurag Sinha as Saru's ex-fiancé and Shraddha Das as Inder's ex-girlfriend.[2]

Climax

Tells the story of a love-hate relationship between Inder, a brooding ex-convict, and Saru, a bookish librarian. The movie explores themes of sacrifice, redemption, and the power of love, culminating in a tragic ending where Saru dies, leaving Inder heartbroken. Here's a more detailed explanation: The Protagonists: The film centers around Saraswati (Saru), a shy and seemingly unlucky librarian, and Inder, a tough and mysterious man who lives next door. Initial Conflict: Saru's family disowns her after a misunderstanding with her father. She seeks help from Inder to transform herself and find a suitable match. Love Blossoms: As Inder helps Saru with her makeover and social life, they begin to fall in love. Tragedy Strikes: Saru is diagnosed with a terminal brain injury after being hit by a stone during a riot. Sacrifice and Devotion: Inder desperately tries to save her, but ultimately, she dies in his arms. Heartbreaking Ending: Inder is left devastated but honors Saru's final wish by burying her under a tree with a headstone that reads "Saraswati Parthasarthy, beloved wife of Inder Parihaar". He promises to remain connected to her spiritually. Themes: The movie explores themes of love, sacrifice, redemption, and the enduring power of love even in the face of tragedy. It also highlights the importance of family and forgiveness. Inspiration: The film is said to be inspired by the story of Shiva and Sati from the Shiva Purana, and also from the novel Love Story by Erich Segal
